# Target Buyer Profile: The Corporate Procurement Director

This document tracks how corporate procurement officers, HR Directors, and CFOs in Nigeria and Anglophone Africa make decisions when choosing a Travel Management Company (TMC).

## 1. What Triggers a Buyer to Look for a New Agency?
Corporate buyers rarely change travel agencies for no reason. They look for a new agency when:
*   **The "Blackout" Crisis:** An executive gets stuck at an airport during an international flight disruption, and the current agency's helpdesk is completely unreachable.
*   **Billing Friction:** The monthly reconciliation reports from the agency are messy, late, or have hidden fees that anger the finance department.
*   **Loss of Trust:** The buyer suspects they are not getting the best negotiated corporate rates or route combinations.

## 2. How the Modern Buyer Searches for a TMC
Before they ever issue a formal RFP (Request for Proposal), they do informal research:
*   **Peer Recommendations:** They ask other Procurement Directors in their professional network (or on LinkedIn) which agency they use.
*   **Search and AI:** Increasingly, they ask search engines or AI tools (like ChatGPT or Gemini): *"Who are the best corporate travel agencies in Nigeria handling multinational clients?"*
*   **Shortlist Creation:** They visit the LinkedIn profiles of the agency founders to see if they understand modern corporate travel complexities, or if they just post generic holiday photos.

## 3. What Corporate Buyers Hate in Travel Proposals
*   **Identical Pitches:** Every agency claims to have "excellent customer service" and "24/7 support." To a buyer, these sound like empty promises.
*   **No West African Context:** Standard proposals often ignore local operational realities (like currency exchange challenges, immigration timeline delays, and regional airline scheduling quirks).
*   **Lack of Technology:** They reject agencies that still rely entirely on manual, slow email workflows instead of structured, efficient technology.
