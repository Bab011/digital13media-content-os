# Pillar 2: Digital13Media Brand Voice

## Voice in One Sentence
Quiet authority delivered through cinematic specificity — the voice of someone who has been inside the room and is now describing what they saw, clearly, without performance.

## The Ten Rules (Non-Negotiable)
1.  **Second-person by default:** Speak directly to the agency owner ("you"). Never refer to them in the third person as "agencies" or "travel businesses."
2.  **Sentence length variation:** Short sentences build tension and rhythm; longer sentences let an idea breathe. Never use three long sentences or five short ones in a row without variation.
3.  **Specific openings:** Always open in a specific city, with a specific person, at a specific moment (e.g., "A procurement officer in Abuja. A Tuesday morning. A laptop screen."). Avoid broad opening statements.
4.  **Inevitable metaphors:** Metaphors must feel inevitable and natural, not clever or forced.
5.  **Data as human stories:** Never drop statistics in isolation. Weave data into human moments so they carry meaning.
6.  **Plain language:** Use contractions (like "don't" or "I'd") and straightforward language. Never sound stiff, academic, or corporate.
7.  **Quiet authority:** Be confident without performing confidence. State facts directly without announcing their importance.
8.  **Consequence with precision:** Name the costs of inaction clearly without sounding alarmist. Do not use exclamation marks or capital letters for emphasis.
9.  **Deliberate lines:** Every single sentence must carry information or build rhythm. If a line is just filler, delete it.
10. **Ends with insight:** Every piece must close with a reflection or insight that leaves the reader thinking, not a hard pitch or call to action.

## The Banned List
*   **Banned Words:** omnichannel, synergy, leverage, game-changing, skyrocket, modern solutions, budget, cheap, "in today's digital landscape", "as a travel professional", "stay relevant", disruptive, unlock, empower.
*   **Banned Constructions:** Openings that look like generic newsletters, sentences showing empty enthusiasm ("We're excited to..."), empty flattery, or hard sales pitches disguised as insights.

## Key Sentence Architecture
*   **The Rhythm Pattern:** Short. Short. Medium — with a turn. Pause. Long explanatory sentence.
*   **The Isolation Move:** Use single-line paragraphs sparingly (maximum twice per piece) to highlight critical points.
*   **Repeat-for-Emphasis:** Repeat a key word across adjacent sentences for structural emphasis (e.g., "The expertise was real. The visibility was not.").
*   **The Em Dash:** Use an em dash (—) to add consequence or qualification without breaking the reading flow.
