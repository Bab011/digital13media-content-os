# Pillar 3: Digital13Media Visual Identity

## The Mood in One Sentence
Quiet luxury. The restraint of a well-run private members' club. African in its warmth, international in its ambition. Every visual element earns its place.

## The Colour System
These five colors define the Digital13Media visual world. They do not compete; they work in sequence.
*   **Midnight Navy (`#1B2B4B`):** Primary background. Foundation. The authority colour.
*   **Antique Gold (`#C09A5B`):** Accent. Headings. The signal of credibility and craft. Used on section divider lines and labels, never as a full background fill.
*   **Warm Parchment (`#F7F3ED`):** Light background. Breathing space. Warmth without softness.
*   **Deep Charcoal (`#1A1A2E`):** Body text. Grounded, readable, serious.
*   **Quiet White (`#FAFAF8`):** Canvas. Margins. The space that makes everything else land.

## Typography Rule
Georgia headlines. Arial body. Never reversed. Never mixed within a single element. These two typefaces have different jobs, and they do not swap.
*   **Georgia (Primary Typeface):** Used for Headlines, Pull Quotes, Article Titles, and Section Headers. Signals permanence and editorial authority.
*   **Arial (Secondary Typeface):** Used for Body Copy, Bullets, Labels, Captions, and Data Tables. Clean, functional, and deferential to the message.

## Layout & Composition Principles
*   **Breathing space is not wasted space:** Margins must be generous. Crowding signals insecurity—the need to fill every centimetre with content. Restraint signals confidence.
*   **One dominant element per section:** Each layout section must have only one thing carrying the weight (one headline, one pull quote, or one image).
*   **Navy as stage:** When navy appears as a background (such as for section headers or highlighted boxes), it frames the most important content. Used sparingly.

## Imagery & Photography Direction
*   **Real over staged; specific over generic:** Use actual locations and real operational specificity (e.g., an actual Lagos morning, an actual GDS ticketing terminal). Avoid generic office stock photos.
*   **Word as design:** In social media graphics, avoid decorative shapes. Let the text carry the visual weight.

## What This Brand Is Not (Strictly Banned)
*   **Not maximalist:** No competing visual elements, decorative borders, or color overload.
*   **Not cute:** No rounded corners, playful icons, or illustrations that soften the authority.
*   **Not corporate grey:** No standard, sterile PowerPoint aesthetics.
*   **Not startup:** No gradients, high-contrast neons, or "disruptive" tech language.
*   **Not generic African:** No kente patterns used purely as background decoration. Restraint and specificity are the point.
